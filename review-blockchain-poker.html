Overview

Blockchain Poker is the most accessible crypto poker platform available — it requires no account registration, no email, and no personal information. Simply send Bitcoin, Bitcoin Cash, or Bitcoin SV to a deposit address and you're playing. The platform has been running since 2015 and appeals to players who value privacy and simplicity above all else.

While it lacks the feature richness of full-service poker rooms, Blockchain Poker excels at delivering a frictionless poker experience. The tables are straightforward Texas Hold'em, the stakes range from free to moderate, and withdrawals are instant to your crypto wallet. For casual poker players who want to play with crypto without any signup process, Blockchain Poker is unmatched.

Bonus & Promotions

Blockchain Poker doesn't offer traditional deposit bonuses, but it provides free satoshis through a faucet system — we received 100 satoshis (roughly $0.07 at current BTC prices) on our first visit, enough to sit at the lowest-stakes table. The faucet refreshes periodically, allowing new players to keep playing for free while they learn the platform. It's not going to build anyone's bankroll, but it removes the barrier to entry entirely.

Regular freeroll tournaments run every few hours with prize pools of 10,000-50,000 satoshis. There's no VIP program, no rakeback, and no loyalty rewards — Blockchain Poker's value proposition is the opposite of bonus-driven: ultra-low rake (2.5% capped at 2,000 satoshis) and zero fees on everything. Compared to CoinPoker's 5% rake or ACR's 5% + tournament fees, the cost savings over time are meaningful for micro-stakes regulars.

Games & Traffic

Blockchain Poker offers No-Limit Texas Hold'em exclusively — no PLO, no Short Deck, no Stud. The game selection is intentionally minimal, and the platform has shown no interest in expanding formats. What it does offer plays cleanly: 6-max and 9-max cash tables at stakes from 1/2 satoshis (essentially free) up to 500/1,000 satoshis (around $3.50/$7 at current BTC prices).

Player traffic is modest but consistent, with peak hours seeing 400-600 concurrent players. We counted 8-12 active tables during European evening peak and 3-5 during off-hours. The tables are populated at lower stakes, making it ideal for recreational players and Bitcoin enthusiasts who want a quick session without the overhead of a full poker client.

Crypto Payments

Blockchain Poker supports Bitcoin (BTC), Bitcoin Cash (BCH), and Bitcoin SV (BSV). Deposits have no minimum — any amount sent to your deposit address is credited after one blockchain confirmation. Withdrawals are instant and free.

The platform operates entirely on-chain, meaning your funds are always in your control until you sit at a table. This is as close to 'trustless' as crypto poker gets.

Security & Fairness

Blockchain Poker operates without a traditional gaming license, which some players may view as a risk. However, the platform's no-account model means minimal personal data is at risk. The platform has operated without incident since 2015.

The provably fair system allows players to verify hand outcomes. The platform's simplicity also means a smaller attack surface compared to complex full-service platforms.

Mobile Experience

Blockchain Poker works in any mobile browser with no app required. The interface is simple and responsive, making it easy to play a quick session on your phone. The minimalist design loads quickly even on slower connections.

Customer Support

Support is available via email and through a small but active Reddit community (r/blockchainpoker). Given the platform's simplicity, most issues are straightforward — deposit not credited, withdrawal pending, table display issue. We sent a test email and received a response within 4 hours, which is acceptable given the platform's lean team. There's no live chat, phone support, or ticket system.

Game Formats & Stakes

Blockchain Poker operates exclusively in No-Limit Texas Hold'em, available in 6-max and 9-max ring game formats. All tables use satoshi-denominated stakes, which means your bankroll fluctuates with Bitcoin's price in fiat terms. The lowest stake is 1/2 satoshis, effectively free play, while the highest regular tables run at 500/1,000 satoshis. During special events, higher-stakes tables at 1,000/2,000 satoshis occasionally appear.

Sit-and-go tournaments are available with buy-ins from 1,000 to 100,000 satoshis. Scheduled tournaments run 3-4 times daily with varying structures. There's no multi-table tournament series, no Spin & Go format, and no fast-fold poker. If you want anything beyond straightforward Hold'em ring games and basic SNGs, this isn't your platform. But for players who just want to sit down and play no-frills poker with Bitcoin, the simplicity is the product.

Player Traffic & Peak Hours

Peak concurrent players hover between 400 and 600, making Blockchain Poker the smallest of the crypto poker rooms we review. The busiest period is 18:00-00:00 UTC, aligning with European and US East Coast evening hours. During peak, you'll find 8-12 active cash tables (mostly at lower stakes) and 1-2 SNGs filling. Off-peak traffic drops to 150-250 players with 3-5 active tables.

Weekend traffic bumps by roughly 20-30% over weekdays. The player base is predominantly Bitcoin enthusiasts rather than serious poker players, which keeps the competition level soft. We found the 10/20 and 25/50 satoshi tables to have the most consistent action. Higher-stakes tables at 100/200+ satoshis often require waiting for a game to start, particularly on weekdays. The small but loyal community means you'll see familiar screen names frequently.

Tournament Schedule

Blockchain Poker's tournament schedule is bare-bones compared to CoinPoker or ACR. Daily offerings include 3-4 freerolls (10,000-50,000 satoshi prize pools), a couple of low buy-in SNGs, and one scheduled tournament with a buy-in around 25,000-50,000 satoshis. There are no flagship Sunday events, no multi-day series, and no satellite structures.

The freerolls are the highlight for new players and serve as the primary way to build a bankroll on the platform without depositing. Fields are small (20-60 players), so the odds of finishing in the money are reasonable. Tournament structures tend to be fast — 5-minute blind levels with short starting stacks (100BB) — which keeps events under an hour. For players seeking deep-stacked, skill-intensive MTT action, CoinPoker or GGPoker are better options. Blockchain Poker's tournaments are designed for quick, casual play.

Rake Structure & Rakeback

This is where Blockchain Poker quietly excels. The rake is 2.5% on cash games, capped at 2,000 satoshis — roughly half the industry standard of 5%. At micro stakes, the cap effectively means most pots are raked at well below 2.5%. Tournament fees are similarly low at 5-8% of the buy-in, compared to the 10% standard at most rooms.

There's no rakeback program, no VIP system, and no loyalty rewards. The value comes from the structural cost savings rather than promotional kickbacks. For a micro-stakes player generating 100,000 satoshis in rake per month on other platforms, the same volume on Blockchain Poker would cost roughly 50,000 satoshis — a 50% savings. The absence of withdrawal fees (zero on all transactions) further reduces the effective cost of playing. It's an honest, low-overhead model that suits its anonymous, no-frills identity.

Software & HUD Compatibility

Blockchain Poker runs entirely in the browser — no download, no installation, no app. You visit the site, pick a stake, and sit down. The interface is deliberately minimalist: a green felt table, player avatars, chip stacks, and a bet slider. Load times are under 2 seconds even on mobile networks. The trade-off is zero customization — no table themes, no card back options, no resize controls.

HUD software is not supported and cannot be used since the platform runs in-browser with no hand history export. There are no built-in statistics beyond your current session results and chip stack. For serious players who track their performance meticulously, this is a non-starter. For the platform's target audience — casual Bitcoin users who want a quick poker session — the zero-setup browser experience is the entire value proposition. We tested on Chrome, Firefox, and Safari on both desktop and mobile, and the experience was consistent across all browsers.
