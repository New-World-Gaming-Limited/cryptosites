Overview

SwC Poker (formerly Seals with Clubs) is one of the original Bitcoin poker rooms, operating since 2012. The platform holds a unique place in crypto poker history and continues to serve a dedicated community of Bitcoin poker enthusiasts. SwC offers multiple poker variants including Hold'em, Omaha, and mixed games.

While the platform is smaller than newer competitors like CoinPoker, SwC Poker has maintained its community through a focus on the core poker experience, anonymous play, and Bitcoin-only transactions. The platform is particularly popular among US players who face limited options in regulated online poker.

Bonus & Promotions

SwC Poker offers a 200% first deposit bonus — the highest percentage match among crypto poker rooms we've reviewed. Deposit 100,000 satoshis and receive 200,000 satoshis in bonus funds, released incrementally as you generate rake. The release rate is approximately 5x rake for $1 of bonus, which means a micro-stakes regular can clear the full amount within 2-3 months of consistent play.

The platform runs freerolls every 2-3 hours with prize pools of 25,000-100,000 satoshis. The VIP rakeback program returns 10-36% based on monthly volume, paid weekly in Bitcoin directly to your account. SwC also runs occasional special promotions tied to Bitcoin price milestones or community events — the last one awarded 500,000 satoshis across a leaderboard during a BTC all-time high celebration.

Games & Traffic

SwC Poker offers an unusually broad format selection for its size: No-Limit Hold'em, Pot-Limit Omaha, 7-Card Stud, Razz, Stud Hi-Lo, 2-7 Triple Draw, and 8-Game mixed rotations. The variety rivals ACR and exceeds every other crypto-native room. Cash games run at satoshi-denominated stakes from 1/2 sats to 500/1,000 sats, with most action concentrated at the 5/10 to 50/100 sat levels.

Player traffic is modest, with peak concurrent players around 150-200. Low-stakes NL Hold'em tables are the most consistently active (3-6 tables during peak), while Stud and mixed games may require waiting or posting in the lobby. Despite the small numbers, the community is dedicated — many players have been on SwC for years, creating a tight-knit but competitive micro-stakes ecosystem.

Crypto Payments

SwC Poker is Bitcoin-only, which is both its strength (simplicity) and limitation (no altcoin support). Deposits are credited after blockchain confirmation, and withdrawals are processed typically within 30 minutes.

The platform uses satoshi-denominated tables, making it intuitive for Bitcoin users. No fiat conversion is offered.

Security & Fairness

SwC Poker operates without a traditional gaming license. The original platform (Seals with Clubs) experienced a security incident in 2015, after which it was rebuilt as SwC Poker with improved security measures. The platform now uses modern encryption and security practices.

Anonymous play is a core feature — no personal information or KYC is required.

Mobile Experience

SwC Poker offers a downloadable client for desktop. The mobile experience is limited, with basic browser access available but not optimized for mobile play.

Customer Support

Support is available via email and in-client messaging. The community is tight-knit, and help is often available through player forums and the active Discord channel (500+ members). We sent a support email about a withdrawal delay and received a response within 3 hours. The Discord community provided an answer to a game-rule question within minutes. For a platform this size, the support is adequate if not instant.

Game Formats & Stakes

SwC Poker's format selection punches well above its weight for a room with 200 peak players. The full list includes NL Hold'em, PLO, 7-Card Stud, Stud Hi-Lo, Razz, 2-7 Triple Draw, Badugi, and rotating 8-Game mixed games. All tables are denominated in satoshis. Cash game stakes range from 1/2 sats (essentially free play) to 500/1,000 sats, with the 25/50 and 50/100 sat levels seeing the most consistent action. Heads-up tables are available but rarely populated outside of scheduled heads-up challenges.

Sit-and-go formats include standard 6-max and 9-max with buy-ins from 5,000 to 200,000 satoshis. The format variety is a genuine differentiator — SwC is the only crypto-native room where you can reliably find Razz or 2-7 Triple Draw, even if you sometimes need to sit and wait for a table to fill. For mixed-game enthusiasts who also want Bitcoin-only transactions, SwC is essentially the only option in the crypto poker ecosystem.

Player Traffic & Peak Hours

SwC Poker's peak traffic sits at 150-200 concurrent players, making it the second-smallest room we review after Blockchain Poker. The busiest window is 22:00-04:00 UTC, covering US evening hours where the majority of SwC's player base is located. During peak, expect 4-8 active NL Hold'em tables, 1-3 PLO tables, and occasional Stud or mixed game tables.

Off-peak traffic drops to 50-80 players with 2-4 active tables. Weekend traffic is 20-30% higher than weekday averages. The small player pool means you will encounter the same regulars repeatedly, which has pros and cons — you can develop reads on opponents, but they'll develop reads on you too. SwC has never had the traffic volumes of CoinPoker or ACR, and at this stage, the community is essentially a niche group of Bitcoin purists who prioritize privacy and Bitcoin-native transactions over player pool size.

Tournament Schedule

SwC runs a modest but consistent tournament schedule. Daily offerings include 4-6 freerolls with 25,000-100,000 satoshi prize pools, a daily low buy-in event (10,000 sats), and a nightly feature (50,000-100,000 sat buy-in) with a 500,000-1,000,000 sat guarantee. The Sunday Championship is the weekly highlight with a 250,000 sat buy-in and a 5,000,000 sat (roughly $3,500) guarantee.

Tournament fields are small — 15-50 players for most events, occasionally reaching 80-100 for freerolls and the Sunday Championship. The small fields mean shorter time commitments (1-3 hours for most events) and reasonable payout odds, but the prizes are modest compared to CoinPoker's CSOP or ACR's Venom. Satellite structures exist for the Sunday Championship, starting at 5,000 sats. The tournament structures have improved over the years, with most events now using 10-minute blind levels and 150-200BB starting stacks — a significant improvement from SwC's early days of fast, shallow structures.

Rake Structure & Rakeback

SwC Poker charges a 2.5% rake on cash games, capped at varying levels by stake — roughly 1,000-5,000 satoshis depending on the table. This is among the lowest rake rates in online poker, matching Blockchain Poker and undercutting the 5% standard at CoinPoker, GGPoker, and ACR. Tournament fees are 5-10% of buy-ins.

The VIP rakeback program returns 10-36% based on monthly volume across four tiers. At the highest tier (achievable with consistent daily play at 50/100+ sats), the 36% rakeback combined with the already-low 2.5% rake creates an effective rake of about 1.6% — possibly the cheapest cash game poker available anywhere online. For a player generating 500,000 satoshis in monthly rake, the VIP program returns 180,000 satoshis. Rakeback is paid weekly in Bitcoin directly, with no token conversions or withdrawal restrictions.

Software & HUD Compatibility

SwC Poker uses a downloadable Java-based client for Windows, macOS, and Linux. The client's visual design is dated compared to modern platforms — it looks and feels like a 2012-era poker room, which it essentially is. Table customization is limited to basic background colors. The software is stable but not particularly responsive; table animations can lag slightly when multi-tabling 4+ tables. The client supports up to 8 simultaneous tables.

HUD compatibility is a mixed bag. SwC generates hand history files that can be imported into PokerTracker 4 and Hold'em Manager 3, but the integration isn't officially supported and occasionally requires hand history converter tools. Real-time HUD overlays work for NL Hold'em and PLO but may not parse correctly for Stud and mixed game variants. The built-in statistics are minimal — hand count and session results only. For HUD-dependent grinders, SwC is functional but requires more setup than ACR, where tracking software integrates seamlessly. The open-source community has built unofficial tools for SwC hand history parsing, available through poker forums and GitHub.
