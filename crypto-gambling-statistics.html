Key Growth Trends

Altcoin Adoption

USDT, Solana, and Litecoin are all gaining share from Bitcoin and Ethereum. Cheaper fees and faster transactions are the main drivers. SOL grew from near-zero to 3.1% in two years.

No-KYC Growth

No-KYC platforms are growing at roughly 2x the rate of KYC-required platforms. Privacy concerns and faster onboarding drive this trend, particularly in Brazil and parts of Asia.

Provably Fair Adoption

The share of platforms offering provably fair games grew from 45% in 2022 to over 65% in 2024. Players are increasingly choosing platforms where fairness can be independently verified.

Mobile-First Shift

62% of global crypto gambling traffic comes from mobile devices. In Brazil this reaches 82%. Platforms with poor mobile experiences are losing ground to mobile-optimized competitors.

Sources: Techopedia, SOFTSWISS, CoinGecko

Frequently Asked Questions

How big is the crypto gambling market?

The global crypto gambling market processed over $250 billion in annual wagers in 2024, according to Techopedia estimates. The market grew 35.9% year-over-year driven by Bitcoin casino adoption, stablecoin deposits, and mobile expansion.

Which country has the most crypto gamblers?

Brazil and the United States together account for about 52.6% of global crypto gambling traffic, according to SOFTSWISS 2024 data. Brazil has the highest mobile gambling rate at 82% of sessions.

Is Bitcoin still the most used crypto for gambling?

Yes, Bitcoin still holds over 50% of crypto gambling deposits. However, its share is declining as stablecoins (USDT) and cheaper networks (Solana, Litecoin) gain ground.

What is the growth rate of crypto gambling?

The crypto gambling market grew 35.9% in 2024. Analysts project continued double-digit growth through 2026, driven by no-KYC adoption, mobile expansion, and new altcoin integrations.

How many people gamble with crypto?

Exact global figures are hard to verify due to the pseudonymous nature of crypto. Industry estimates put active crypto gambling accounts in the tens of millions globally, with Brazil, the US, and several Asian markets leading.
