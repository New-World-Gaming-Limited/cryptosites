Quick Verdict

Ledger is the better choice for users who want maximum coin support and Bluetooth connectivity for mobile management. Trezor is ideal for open-source advocates who value full code transparency and a simpler interface. Both are excellent hardware wallets — the choice often comes down to whether you prioritise Ledger's broader ecosystem or Trezor's open-source philosophy. For first-time hardware wallet buyers, either is a massive security upgrade over keeping crypto on an exchange.

Supported Assets

Ledger supports over 5,500 coins and tokens through its Ledger Live software and third-party integrations, making it the most comprehensive hardware wallet for multi-chain users. Trezor supports around 1,400 coins, covering all major assets but lagging on newer chains and tokens. If you hold a diverse portfolio including DeFi tokens and NFTs, Ledger's broader support is a significant advantage.

Security Architecture

Both wallets use certified secure element chips to protect private keys. Ledger uses a proprietary operating system (BOLOS) running on its secure element, while Trezor's firmware is fully open-source and auditable by anyone. This is the fundamental philosophical divide: Ledger's closed-source approach potentially limits peer review but benefits from hardware-level certification (CC EAL5+). Trezor's open-source code has been extensively audited by the community, providing a different kind of trust assurance. Both support passphrase protection and recovery seed backup.

Hardware & Design

Ledger's current lineup includes the Nano S Plus (USB-C, affordable) and the Nano X (Bluetooth, premium). The Bluetooth capability on the Nano X allows management from mobile devices without a cable. Trezor offers the Model One (budget, USB) and Model T (colour touchscreen, USB-C). Trezor's touchscreen makes PIN entry and seed verification more intuitive. Build quality is solid on both brands, with Ledger feeling slightly more premium due to its metal casing.

Software & Ecosystem

Ledger Live is a polished companion app for desktop and mobile that supports portfolio tracking, staking, swapping, and buying crypto directly. Trezor Suite offers similar features with a clean, open-source interface. Both integrate with popular wallets like MetaMask for DeFi interactions. Ledger's partnership ecosystem is broader, with direct integrations into more DeFi protocols and NFT marketplaces.

Pricing & Value

The Ledger Nano S Plus starts around $79, while the Nano X is approximately $149. Trezor Model One starts at about $69, and the Model T at around $219. For the most popular models (Nano S Plus vs Model One), pricing is comparable. The Nano X commands a premium for Bluetooth functionality, while the Trezor Model T costs more due to its colour touchscreen. Both frequently run promotions and bundle deals that reduce the effective price.

Final Verdict: Ledger Wins (9.3 vs 9.0)

Based on our comprehensive testing, Ledger takes the overall edge in this comparison. However, both platforms are excellent choices — your ideal pick depends on your specific priorities.
