Bonus & Promotions

JackPoker's welcome offer combines a 100% deposit match up to $500 with 50 free spins on selected casino slots — reflecting the platform's poker-casino hybrid model. The poker bonus portion releases through rake contribution at a 15x rate, meaning $15 in rake unlocks $1 of bonus. A regular $0.25/$0.50 player should clear the full $500 within 3-4 months. The 50 free spins are credited immediately and carry a standard 30x wagering requirement on casino games only.

Ongoing promotions include weekly freeroll tournaments (Tuesday and Thursday, $500 prize pool each), monthly rake races with $5,000 in prizes, and a 4-tier VIP program returning 10-30% cashback. JackPoker also runs occasional "Double Rake Race" weekends where all rake contributions count 2x toward leaderboard standings. Compared to CoinPoker's CSOP or GGPoker's WSOP Online, the promotional value is modest — but for the platform's size and recreational focus, the offerings are appropriate.

Games & Traffic

JackPoker focuses on NL Hold'em and PLO across cash games, SNGs, and scheduled tournaments. A fast-fold format ("Boost Poker") is available at stakes from $0.02/$0.05 to $1/$2. Cash game stakes range from $0.01/$0.02 micro tables to $2/$5, with the majority of action concentrated at $0.05/$0.10 through $0.50/$1. Higher stakes above $2/$5 are not offered.

Peak traffic sits around 5,000 concurrent players, with the busiest window during European evening hours (17:00-23:00 UTC). We counted 10-18 active cash tables during peak and 4-8 during off-peak. The player base is predominantly recreational, drawn from JackPoker's casino crossover audience. Table VPIP averages run 30-40% at low stakes, significantly higher than on GGPoker (22-28%) or ACR (24-30%), making this one of the softest player pools in crypto poker.

Crypto Payments

JackPoker supports Bitcoin, Ethereum, Tether, and Litecoin. Deposits confirm within minutes, and withdrawals to crypto wallets are processed in under 2 hours on average — faster than many larger competitors. The minimum deposit is $10 equivalent.

Security & Fairness

Operating under a Curacao license, JackPoker employs SSL encryption, two-factor authentication, and RNG certification. While the regulatory oversight is less rigorous than top-tier jurisdictions, the platform has maintained a clean track record since launch in 2020.

Mobile Experience

JackPoker offers a browser-based mobile interface that works on both iOS and Android. While there is no dedicated app, the mobile web experience is responsive and functional for single-tabling. Serious multi-tablers will prefer the desktop client.

Customer Support

Support is available via live chat and email. In our testing, live chat connected in 12 minutes — slower than GGPoker (3 min) or ACR (3 min) but acceptable for a smaller platform. The agent handled a basic withdrawal inquiry competently. Email support responded within 18 hours. The FAQ section is adequate, covering deposit methods, game rules, and bonus terms. There's no phone support, Telegram group, or community forum.

Game Formats & Stakes

JackPoker's core formats are NL Hold'em and PLO, available in cash game, SNG, and MTT formats. The Boost Poker fast-fold variant runs at 4 stake levels ($0.02/$0.05 through $1/$2) and provides a decent option for players who want high hand volume without waiting for a new table deal. SNG formats include standard 6-max and 9-max with buy-ins from $1 to $50, plus jackpot SNGs (called "Lucky Spin") with 3-player format and multipliers up to 500x.

Cash game stakes run $0.01/$0.02 to $2/$5 — a narrower range than CoinPoker ($0.01/$0.02 to $5/$10) or ACR ($0.01/$0.02 to $25/$50). There are no mixed games, no Stud variants, and no Short Deck. The format selection is limited but covers what 90% of recreational players actually play. Tournament buy-ins range from freerolls to $100, with guarantees capped at $10,000 for the Sunday highlight. For players who want variety, this is a constraint; for those who just want Hold'em and PLO in a soft ecosystem, the offering is sufficient.

Player Traffic & Peak Hours

JackPoker's peak traffic of 5,000 concurrent players puts it in the mid-range of crypto poker rooms — above SwC Poker (200) and Blockchain Poker (500) but well below CoinPoker (5,000+) and GGPoker (150,000+). The busiest window is 17:00-23:00 UTC, aligning with European evening hours. During peak, NL Hold'em tables are well-populated at all available stakes, and PLO runs 3-5 active tables.

Off-peak traffic drops to 1,500-2,500 players with 4-8 active tables. The weekend bump is noticeable at roughly 25% above weekday averages. The casino-poker hybrid model generates a steady stream of recreational crossover players — we observed players sitting down at poker tables directly from casino sessions, often playing loose-aggressive styles typical of casino converts. This crossover effect is JackPoker's biggest competitive advantage: the games are consistently softer than at dedicated poker rooms.

Tournament Schedule

JackPoker's tournament schedule is modest but functional. Daily offerings include 3-4 freerolls ($100-$250 prize pools), a $1,000 GTD evening event ($11 buy-in), and 2-3 turbo events throughout the day. The Sunday Special ($10,000 GTD, $55 buy-in) is the weekly highlight, regularly attracting 150-250 entries. Monthly events include a $25,000 GTD Main Event ($109 buy-in) and a Progressive Knockout series.

Satellite structures are basic: $1 satellites feed into the Sunday Special, and $5 satellites qualify for the monthly Main Event. There are no multi-step qualification paths to high buy-in events since JackPoker doesn't run events above $109 buy-in. For tournament grinders seeking major guarantees, CoinPoker's CSOP ($1M+) or GGPoker's WSOP Online are clearly superior. JackPoker's tournament appeal is for recreational players who want a Sunday event without a massive time commitment — most daily events finish within 90 minutes, and the Sunday Special wraps in under 4 hours.

Rake Structure & Rakeback

JackPoker charges a 5% rake on cash games, capped at $2 for most stakes — slightly lower than the $3 cap standard at CoinPoker, GGPoker, and ACR. At the $0.01/$0.02 level, the cap drops to $0.25. Tournament fees run 10% of buy-in, which is standard. The Boost Poker fast-fold format charges the same rake as regular tables, with no premium for the speed format.

The 4-tier VIP program (Bronze, Silver, Gold, Platinum) returns 10%, 15%, 20%, and 30% cashback respectively. Platinum requires $5,000+ in monthly rake, achievable for dedicated mid-stakes grinders. At Platinum with the $2 rake cap, the effective rake after 30% cashback is 3.5% capped at $1.40 — competitive but not exceptional. JackPoker does not offer CHP-style token discounts or flat-rate rakeback deals. Cash back is paid weekly in your chosen cryptocurrency. For pure rakeback value, BC Poker (90%), CoinPoker's CHP discount (33%), and SwC Poker's low base rake (2.5%) all offer better returns. JackPoker's VIP program is middle-of-the-road.

Software & HUD Compatibility

JackPoker offers a downloadable desktop client for Windows and a browser-based interface for macOS, iOS, and Android. The Windows client is functional and stable, supporting up to 6 simultaneous tables with auto-tile layout. Table customization includes 4 background themes, 3 card designs, and adjustable bet slider sensitivity. The interface is clean and beginner-friendly — action buttons are large, bet sizes are clearly displayed, and the lobby is organized intuitively.

External HUD software is not officially supported, and JackPoker does not export hand history files in standard format. Some third-party tools claim compatibility through screen-scraping methods, but these are unreliable and potentially against the platform's terms of service. The built-in statistics cover basic session data: hands played, win/loss, and time at table. For players who rely on HUD overlays, ACR is the clear alternative. JackPoker's software approach mirrors the broader industry trend toward recreational protection — limiting data-mining tools keeps the playing field level between casual players and experienced grinders, which is consistent with the platform's identity as a recreational-focused room.
