Overview

JackPoker is a newer entrant to the crypto poker scene, launched in 2020 with a focus on recreational players and a casino-poker hybrid model. The platform runs on its own independent network, which means a smaller player pool but also softer competition — a trade-off that appeals to players looking for profitable games rather than maximum liquidity.

Licensed in Curacao, JackPoker accepts Bitcoin, Ethereum, Tether, and Litecoin. The platform differentiates itself by combining poker with casino games, offering free spins as part of its welcome package alongside the traditional poker bonus. Withdrawal speeds are competitive, typically under 2 hours for crypto payouts.

Bonus & Promotions

JackPoker's welcome offer combines a 100% deposit match up to $500 with 50 free spins on selected casino games. The poker bonus has a 25x wagering requirement, which is standard for hybrid platforms. Regular promotions include weekly freeroll tournaments, rake races, and a VIP program that rewards consistent play with cashback and exclusive tournament access.

Games & Traffic

The game selection includes No-Limit Hold'em and Pot-Limit Omaha across cash games and tournaments. JackPoker also offers Sit & Go formats and fast-fold poker. Cash game stakes range from $0.01/$0.02 to $2/$5. Tournament guarantees are modest compared to major networks, but the recreational player base means games tend to be softer. Peak traffic is around 5,000 concurrent players.

Crypto Payments

JackPoker supports Bitcoin, Ethereum, Tether, and Litecoin. Deposits confirm within minutes, and withdrawals to crypto wallets are processed in under 2 hours on average — faster than many larger competitors. The minimum deposit is $10 equivalent.

Security & Fairness

Operating under a Curacao license, JackPoker employs SSL encryption, two-factor authentication, and RNG certification. While the regulatory oversight is less rigorous than top-tier jurisdictions, the platform has maintained a clean track record since launch in 2020.

Mobile Experience

JackPoker offers a browser-based mobile interface that works on both iOS and Android. While there is no dedicated app, the mobile web experience is responsive and functional for single-tabling. Serious multi-tablers will prefer the desktop client.

Customer Support

Support is available via live chat and email. Response times are reasonable, though the support team is smaller than at major operators. The FAQ section covers common issues adequately.
