Understanding Crypto Network Fees

Every cryptocurrency transaction requires a network fee paid to validators or miners who process and confirm your transaction. These fees are separate from any fees the casino charges (most charge nothing). The fee goes to the blockchain network, not to CryptoBets.com or the casino.

Fee amounts fluctuate based on network demand. The estimates shown are based on average conditions in early 2026 and may differ from live network rates. For real-time fee data, check a blockchain explorer before sending large amounts.

Why Fees Matter for Gambling

A $3 Bitcoin fee on a $1,000 deposit is 0.3% and easily acceptable. The same $3 fee on a $20 deposit is 15%, which meaningfully reduces your effective bankroll before you place a single bet. For small deposits, always use a low-fee network.

Withdrawal fees follow the same logic. If you win $50 and pay $3 to withdraw it in Bitcoin, you keep $47 (a 6% exit cost). Using Litecoin or Solana for that same withdrawal costs under $0.10, keeping your winnings intact.

The practical rule: for deposits under $100, use Litecoin, Solana, or USDT on Tron. For deposits above $200, Bitcoin and Ethereum are fine. For deposits where price stability matters, use USDT on any network.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who receives the network fee?

The network fee goes to miners (Bitcoin, Litecoin) or validators (Ethereum, Solana, Tron) who process your transaction. Neither CryptoBets.com nor the casino receives the network fee.

Do casinos charge additional deposit fees?

Most crypto casinos do not charge deposit fees on top of the network fee. The network fee is the only cost for most deposits. Some casinos may charge a small processing fee for certain methods; check the cashier before depositing.

Why does Ethereum have high fees sometimes?

Ethereum fees (gas) rise and fall with network demand. During periods of heavy usage, fees can spike above $50 per transaction. During quiet periods, fees can drop below $1. Check a gas tracker like ethgasstation.info before sending during suspected busy periods.

Is USDT on Tron the same as USDT on Ethereum?

Both represent $1 per token but run on different blockchains. USDT on Tron (TRC-20) has fees under $1. USDT on Ethereum (ERC-20) has higher fees. Always check which network the casino's deposit address uses, as sending to the wrong network can result in lost funds.

What is the cheapest crypto for casino deposits?

Solana (SOL) has the lowest transaction fees, typically under $0.001. Litecoin and USDT on Tron are close behind at around $0.01 and under $1 respectively. Bitcoin and Ethereum are the most expensive for small deposits.
