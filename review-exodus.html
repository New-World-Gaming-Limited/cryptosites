Overview

Exodus is a multi-chain cryptocurrency wallet known for its exceptionally beautiful design and user-friendly interface. Available on desktop (Windows, Mac, Linux), mobile (iOS, Android), and as a browser extension, Exodus makes managing crypto feel premium and intuitive. The wallet supports 300+ cryptocurrencies with built-in exchange, staking, and portfolio tracking.

While Exodus doesn't match MetaMask for DeFi power users or Trust Wallet for chain breadth, it excels in making crypto management accessible and visually appealing. The wallet integrates with Trezor hardware wallets for enhanced security, providing a bridge between user-friendliness and serious security.

Value & Staking

Exodus is free to use. Revenue is generated through the built-in exchange feature, which uses partner services. Staking is available for supported assets including SOL, ADA, ATOM, and others with competitive APY rates.

The Exodus browser extension is also free and provides Web3 connectivity for DeFi and dApp access.

Features & Products

Exodus supports 300+ cryptocurrencies across multiple chains including Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana, Cardano, and more. Core features include portfolio tracking with beautiful charts, built-in exchange, staking, and Trezor hardware wallet integration.

The desktop experience is Exodus's differentiator — the app is beautifully designed with smooth animations, clear data visualization, and an intuitive layout that makes complex crypto management feel simple.

Supported Cryptos

Exodus supports 300+ cryptocurrencies including major coins and popular tokens. Custom token support is available for Ethereum-based tokens. The built-in exchange allows swapping between any supported asset pair.

While the selection is smaller than Trust Wallet or MetaMask, Exodus curates its supported assets to ensure quality and reliability.

Security

Exodus is a non-custodial hot wallet with keys stored locally on the user's device. The wallet supports biometric authentication and encryption. A key differentiator is the Trezor integration, which allows Exodus users to secure their keys on a hardware device while maintaining the Exodus interface.

Exodus is not open source, which means independent security auditing is limited. For maximum security, using Exodus with a Trezor device is recommended.

Mobile Experience

Exodus offers a polished mobile app for iOS and Android that mirrors the desktop experience. The app is beautifully designed and provides full portfolio management, staking, and exchange functionality.

Customer Support

Customer support is available via email and an extensive help center. Exodus has a strong reputation for responsive, human support. The team also maintains active social media channels and educational content.

Supported Chains & Tokens

Exodus supports over 300 cryptocurrencies across 50+ blockchain networks, striking a balance between comprehensive coverage and a curated, manageable interface. Major assets are all present — Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana, Cardano, Cosmos, Polygon, Avalanche, and their associated tokens. ERC-20, SPL, and other major token standards are supported, with new assets added regularly through app updates.

Compared to Trust Wallet's 100+ chains and 10M+ tokens, Exodus takes a more selective approach. You won't find every obscure DeFi token here, but the assets that are supported work reliably. In our testing, we held BTC, ETH, SOL, ADA, and a selection of ERC-20 tokens without issues. Exodus automatically detects supported tokens in your wallets, though you may need to manually enable visibility for lesser-known assets.

One Exodus differentiator is its unified desktop and mobile experience. Your portfolio syncs seamlessly between the desktop app (Windows, macOS, Linux) and mobile app (iOS, Android), with the same asset coverage on both platforms. For users who frequently switch between desktop and phone, this cross-platform consistency is a genuine convenience.

DeFi & dApp Integration

Exodus has limited built-in DeFi integration compared to MetaMask or Trust Wallet. There's no embedded DApp browser or direct protocol connections within the app. For DeFi access, Exodus users need to either export their keys to a separate wallet (not recommended) or use the Exodus browser extension, which provides MetaMask-style dApp connectivity for EVM chains.

The Exodus browser extension, launched in 2023, is a significant addition. It connects to Ethereum dApps similarly to MetaMask, supporting Uniswap, OpenSea, Aave, and other protocols. We tested it with Uniswap and Aave on Ethereum and Arbitrum — both worked correctly. However, the extension's user base is much smaller than MetaMask's, and some dApps don't explicitly list Exodus as a supported wallet, requiring you to select "Injected Provider" in the connection modal.

For users who want a simple, self-contained wallet without DeFi complexity, Exodus's approach makes sense. But DeFi power users will find MetaMask or Trust Wallet more capable. Exodus's sweet spot is users who want to buy, hold, swap, and stake — without the rabbit hole of yield farming and liquidity provision.

Swap & Exchange Features

Exodus includes a built-in exchange that lets you swap between any supported assets directly within the app. The exchange uses a spread-based fee model rather than a fixed percentage — meaning the fee is baked into the exchange rate rather than displayed separately. In our testing, we found the effective fee ranged from 1-3% depending on the pair and market conditions, which is higher than using DEXs directly but competitive with other in-app swap services.

The swap experience itself is the most polished of any wallet we tested. Select the source and destination assets, enter the amount, and slide to confirm. The rate is locked for a short window, and the swap typically completes within 5-30 minutes depending on blockchain confirmation times. We swapped BTC to ETH and the exchange completed in 12 minutes. Cross-chain swaps are handled seamlessly — the user doesn't need to understand bridges or wrapped tokens.

Fiat purchases are supported through integrated partners including MoonPay, Ramp, and Sardine. You can buy crypto with a credit card, debit card, or bank transfer directly into your Exodus wallet. While the 2-4% card fees are standard for the industry, Exodus's implementation is among the smoothest — three taps from opening the app to completing a purchase.

Staking & Yield

Exodus supports staking for Solana (approximately 6.5% APY), Cardano (around 3.5% APY), Cosmos (roughly 15% APY), Ethereum (via Everstake, about 3.2% APY), and several other proof-of-stake assets. The staking interface is integrated into the asset's detail page — tap your SOL balance, hit "Stake," choose a validator (or use the default), and confirm. We staked SOL in testing and received our first rewards within the next epoch cycle, about 2-3 days later.

Staking rewards are displayed clearly in the portfolio view, with a breakdown by asset. Compared to staking through a hardware wallet like Ledger, Exodus makes the process simpler at the cost of hot-wallet security trade-offs. For users staking modest amounts (under $5,000), the convenience advantage is real. For larger positions, pairing Exodus with Trezor for hardware-secured staking is the recommended approach — Exodus integrates directly with Trezor devices for this purpose.

Security Architecture

Exodus is a non-custodial hot wallet that stores private keys locally on your device, encrypted with your password. Keys are never transmitted to Exodus's servers. The wallet uses a 12-word recovery phrase for backup, compatible with the BIP-39 standard. However, Exodus is closed-source — the application code is not publicly auditable, which is a legitimate concern for security-conscious users.

The closed-source issue is the most debated aspect of Exodus's security model. Exodus argues that closed source prevents attackers from finding vulnerabilities, while critics counter that open-source code benefits from community auditing (as Trezor and MetaMask demonstrate). Third-party security firms have audited Exodus, but results are not fully published. For users who prioritize code transparency, Trezor or MetaMask are better choices.

On the positive side, Exodus integrates directly with Trezor hardware wallets, letting you secure your Exodus portfolio with hardware-level key storage. This is the configuration we recommend for anyone holding more than $5,000 in Exodus. The integration is seamless — your Trezor-secured assets appear in the Exodus interface alongside any hot-wallet assets, with a shield icon indicating which are hardware-protected. Exodus also supports biometric unlock (Face ID, fingerprint) and auto-lock timeouts for day-to-day security.
