Overview

Americas Cardroom (ACR) is one of the largest poker rooms that accepts US players and supports cryptocurrency deposits. Operating since 2001, ACR is a veteran in the online poker space and has embraced crypto payments to provide easier access for its primarily US-based player pool. The platform is known for massive tournament guarantees and high player traffic.

While ACR isn't a crypto-native platform like CoinPoker or SwC, it has become a popular choice for US poker players who use Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies for deposits and withdrawals. The platform offers a full range of poker variants, sit-and-go games, and scheduled tournaments with some of the largest guaranteed prize pools available to US players.

Bonus & Promotions

ACR offers a 100% first deposit bonus up to $2,000 with a 20:1 release rate — meaning for every $20 in rake/fees, $1 of bonus is released. This is a standard release rate, and a player grinding $1/$2 NL should clear the full $2,000 within 4-6 months. The bonus is released in $1 increments directly to your cash balance, and unused portions expire after 60 days of account inactivity.

The tournament schedule is ACR's biggest draw. The Venom tournament runs quarterly with a $10 million guarantee and a $2,650 buy-in — the largest single-event guarantee available to US players. The Online Super Series (OSS) runs monthly with combined guarantees exceeding $15 million across dozens of events. Regular Sunday majors include the $150K GTD ($215 buy-in) and the $60K GTD ($109 buy-in). The Beat the Pros promotion offers $500-$5,000 bounties when you knock out sponsored players at select tables.

Games & Traffic

ACR offers the widest format variety among US-facing crypto-accepting rooms: No-Limit Hold'em, Pot-Limit Omaha, PLO Hi-Lo, 7-Card Stud, Stud Hi-Lo, Razz, 2-7 Triple Draw, Badugi, and a rotating mixed-game (8-Game) schedule. Cash game stakes range from $0.01/$0.02 micro tables to $25/$50 nosebleed games, with consistent action across all levels up to $2/$5. The Blitz format (fast-fold) adds a speed option for grinders who want high hand volume.

Player traffic is strong with 10,000+ peak concurrent players, making ACR the busiest poker room available to US players. During Sunday peak hours (18:00-02:00 ET), we counted 80+ active cash tables and 30+ running tournaments simultaneously. Multi-tabling up to 16 tables is supported, and the software auto-tiles tables efficiently across monitors. The player pool includes a healthy mix of recreational and professional players, though competition is noticeably tougher than at crypto-native rooms like CoinPoker.

Crypto Payments

ACR accepts Bitcoin, Bitcoin Cash, Ethereum, Litecoin, and Tether for deposits. Crypto deposits are typically credited within 15-30 minutes. Crypto withdrawals are processed within 24-48 hours, which is slower than crypto-native platforms but standard for traditional poker rooms.

The minimum deposit for crypto is $25, and ACR doesn't charge deposit fees. Withdrawal limits and processing times depend on player verification level.

Security & Fairness

ACR operates under a Costa Rica gaming license and has been running since 2001. The platform has faced some controversy over the years regarding bot activity, though the team has made significant investments in anti-fraud and anti-collusion technology.

The platform supports two-factor authentication and employs independent RNG auditing. ACR's long history in the industry, while not without bumps, demonstrates staying power.

Mobile Experience

ACR offers a downloadable mobile app for Android devices and a browser-based option for iOS. The mobile experience covers cash games and tournaments, though the desktop client provides the fullest feature set. The mobile app has improved significantly in recent updates.

Customer Support

Support is available 24/7 via live chat, email, and phone. In our testing, live chat connected in under 3 minutes and the agent resolved a withdrawal status inquiry without needing to escalate. Email responses came within 6 hours. ACR also maintains active forums (2+2, PocketFives) and social media channels where staff engage directly with the community. The phone line is US-based and available during business hours for account issues.

Game Formats & Stakes

ACR's format diversity is unmatched among US-facing poker rooms. The core offerings are NL Hold'em and PLO, but the site also regularly spreads Omaha Hi-Lo, 7-Card Stud, Stud Hi-Lo, Razz, 2-7 Triple Draw, Badugi, and 8-Game mixed rotations. Cash game stakes span the full spectrum: $0.01/$0.02 to $25/$50, with regular action at $0.25/$0.50 through $5/$10. The $10/$20 and $25/$50 tables run intermittently, typically during weekend peaks.

Tournament formats include standard MTTs, turbo and hyper-turbo speeds, rebuy and knockout events, Sit & Gos (6-max, 9-max, heads-up), and Jackpot Sit & Gos with multipliers up to 2,500x. The Blitz fast-fold format is available at stakes from $0.02/$0.05 to $1/$2 and is ACR's answer to PokerStars' Zoom and GGPoker's Rush & Cash. The combination of game variety and consistent traffic at all levels makes ACR the most complete poker room available to US crypto players.

Player Traffic & Peak Hours

ACR's peak traffic exceeds 10,000 concurrent players, with the busiest window between 19:00-02:00 ET (00:00-07:00 UTC). This covers US prime time, and the player pool is predominantly American, though Latin American and European players are present during their respective evening hours. During Sunday peaks, tournament lobbies fill rapidly — the Sunday $150K GTD regularly attracts 800+ entries.

Weekday traffic is roughly 40% lower than weekends but still strong enough to fill cash tables at all stakes below $5/$10. PLO traffic has grown significantly, with 10-15 active tables during peak compared to 3-5 two years ago. The Winning Poker Network, which ACR anchors, serves roughly 70% of its traffic from US IP addresses. Compared to crypto-native rooms, ACR's player pool is 5-20x larger depending on the time of day — the trade-off being slower crypto withdrawals and higher rake.

Tournament Schedule

ACR's tournament schedule is the strongest available to US players. The Venom ($10M GTD, $2,650 buy-in) is the quarterly flagship and typically runs over 3 days with a $1M first prize. The Online Super Series (OSS) delivers $15M+ in combined guarantees monthly across 100+ events. Daily offerings include the $100K GTD ($215 buy-in) at 18:00 ET, the $50K GTD ($109 buy-in) at 20:00 ET, and numerous $5K-$25K GTD events throughout the day.

Satellite structures are extensive: $0.01 Mega Satellites feed into major events through multi-step qualification paths, giving micro-stakes players a genuine shot at $2,650 seats. The Sunday lineup includes 15+ events with combined guarantees exceeding $500K. ACR also runs seasonal series like the Millions Online ($5M GTD Main Event) and the High Five tournament series. For tournament grinders, ACR's schedule justifies accepting the slower crypto withdrawal times — no other US-facing room comes close in guarantee volume.

Rake Structure & Rakeback

ACR charges a 5% rake on cash games, capped at $3 for most stakes and $0.50-$1 at micro levels. Tournament fees run 8-10% of buy-ins. Where ACR differentiates is through two complementary rakeback programs: the Beast and Sit & Crush. The Beast distributes a prize pool based on total cash game rake across all players, while Sit & Crush offers SNG-specific rewards.

Combined with the Elite Benefits VIP program, total rakeback at ACR can reach 27-65% depending on volume tier. The top tier (Diamond) requires $30,000+ in monthly rake, which is achievable only for high-volume multi-tablers. A more realistic mid-tier player generating $2,000-$5,000/month in rake can expect 30-40% back through Beast + Elite Benefits. This is competitive with CoinPoker's CHP discount + cashback but below BC Poker's 90% ceiling. ACR pays rakeback weekly in cash, not tokens — a straightforward advantage over platforms that pay in proprietary cryptocurrency.

Software & HUD Compatibility

ACR's desktop client (Windows and macOS) is the most feature-rich among US-facing rooms. The software supports up to 16 simultaneous tables with auto-tile, cascade, and stack layout options. Table customization includes card designs, backgrounds, avatar selection, and bet sizing presets. The client is stable but resource-heavy — 16-tabling sessions used 2-3 GB of RAM in our testing, so a modern system with 8GB+ RAM is recommended.

HUD compatibility is a major strength. ACR fully supports PokerTracker 4, Hold'em Manager 3, and DriveHUD through dedicated hand history files. Hand histories export automatically in standard format, and the client provides real-time hand-by-hand data to tracking software. For professional grinders who rely on stats like VPIP, PFR, 3-bet%, and positional tendencies, ACR is the best-supported crypto-accepting room. This is a stark contrast to CoinPoker and BC Poker, which block HUDs entirely. The mobile app (Android native, iOS via browser) provides basic functionality but doesn't support HUDs or multi-tabling beyond 4 tables.
