How to Read Bonus Terms

The headline bonus percentage is just the start. Three other numbers matter more: the wagering requirement (how many times you must bet the bonus before withdrawing), the maximum bet limit while wagering (often $5-$10 per spin), and the game contributions (slots usually count 100%, table games may count 10-20%).

A 200% bonus with 40x wagering on deposit+bonus is significantly harder to clear than a 100% bonus with 25x wagering on bonus only. The calculator above models this difference with actual numbers.

When Bonuses Are Not Worth Claiming

If the expected loss to complete wagering exceeds the bonus amount, the bonus has negative expected value. This happens with high wagering requirements (40x+) on large bonuses. Sometimes it is better to play without a bonus so you can withdraw whenever you want without meeting wagering conditions.
