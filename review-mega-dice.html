The lottery draws use provably fair random number generation and offer variable jackpot sizes based on participation.

The platform's accessibility through Telegram lowers the barrier to entry for crypto gambling, as users don't need to visit a separate website or download an app.

Security & Licensing

Mega Dice operates under a Curacao gaming license. As a Telegram-based platform, security is tied to both the platform's infrastructure and Telegram's security model. Standard measures like SSL encryption are implemented for the web interface.

The provably fair system for lottery draws allows verification of results. However, the platform's novelty means limited long-term track record.

Mobile Experience

Mega Dice is primarily accessed through the Telegram app, making it inherently mobile-friendly. The web version is also responsive. The Telegram integration provides a unique, chat-based gaming experience.

Customer Support

Support is available through Telegram (the primary communication channel), as well as email. The community Telegram group provides peer support and updates. During our testing, the Telegram bot responded to account queries within 3 minutes, which is impressive for a platform this size.

For lottery-specific issues — disputed results, delayed payouts, ticket purchase errors — support is handled through a dedicated @MegaDiceSupport Telegram channel rather than the main community group. Email response times averaged around 4 hours in our tests.

Draw Mechanics & Transparency

Mega Dice runs its lottery draws through a provably fair RNG system built on Telegram's bot infrastructure. Each draw generates a server seed before ticket sales close, and the client seed is derived from the combined hash of all ticket purchase transaction IDs in that draw window. After the draw, both seeds are published in the Telegram channel, and players can verify the outcome using the platform's open-source verification tool on GitHub.

Daily mega jackpot draws close at 9:00 PM UTC, with results published within 5 minutes. Mini draws run every 4 hours (6 per day), offering smaller but more frequent prize pools. The Telegram-native approach means draw results are pushed directly to subscribers as messages — no need to check a website or refresh a page. We verified 5 consecutive draws during testing and confirmed the seed-to-result chain was consistent each time. The system is less transparent than fully on-chain lotteries like Lucky Block (since verification requires trusting the published seeds), but the open-source verification script adds a layer of auditability.

Jackpot Sizes & History

Mega Dice's daily mega jackpot starts at a $10,000 guaranteed floor and scales with ticket volume. On busy days, the main jackpot has exceeded $75,000. The largest single payout in Q1 2026 was approximately $120,000 — modest compared to Lucky Block's seven-figure draws but significant for a platform operating primarily through Telegram.

Mini draws (every 4 hours) carry prize pools of $500–$2,000 depending on participation. These smaller, frequent draws are actually the platform's strength — they attract players who prefer regular chances at moderate prizes over long-shot big jackpots. DICE token staking also funds a weekly "Mega Pool" draw with a prize pool sourced from platform revenue. The Q4 2025 Mega Pool peaked at $45,000. Cumulative lottery payouts since launch total approximately $3.2M across all draw types.

Ticket Pricing & Odds

Standard tickets for the daily mega draw cost $1 each — the lowest entry price among crypto lottery platforms we've reviewed. Mini draw tickets cost $0.25 each. Bulk pricing applies: 10 mega tickets for $8 (save $2), 25 for $18 (save $7), and 50 for $30 (save $20). DICE token holders who stake 5,000+ DICE receive 2 free daily mega entries and 1 free mini draw entry.

The mega draw uses a 5-number format from a pool of 1 to 36. Top prize odds (matching all 5) are approximately 1 in 376,992 — much more favorable than traditional national lotteries, reflecting the smaller prize pools. Matching 4 numbers carries odds of roughly 1 in 2,432, and matching 3 numbers drops to approximately 1 in 75. The overall odds of winning any prize tier in the mega draw are about 1 in 12. Mini draws use a simpler 3-number format from 1 to 20, with top odds of 1 in 1,140 and any-prize odds of roughly 1 in 8.

Prize Tiers & Payouts

The mega draw has 4 prize tiers. Tier 1 (5 matches) takes 50% of the pool. Tier 2 (4 matches) takes 20%. Tier 3 (3 matches) takes 20%. Tier 4 (2 matches) takes 10%. If Tier 1 goes unclaimed, the allocation rolls over to the next day's draw. Mini draws have 2 tiers: 3 matches (70% of pool) and 2 matches (30% of pool), with no rollover — unclaimed pools are redistributed.

Payouts are processed automatically within 15 minutes of the draw. Winnings are credited to the player's Mega Dice wallet in the cryptocurrency of their choice — BTC, ETH, SOL, and USDT are the most popular withdrawal options. We won a Tier 3 prize during testing and received the USDT payout in 8 minutes. There's no manual claim process; winners are notified via Telegram DM and funds appear in their balance immediately. Minimum withdrawal is $5 equivalent, which is reasonable given the low ticket prices.

Lottery Responsible Gambling

Mega Dice offers basic responsible gambling controls through the Telegram bot interface. Players can set daily ticket purchase limits by messaging the bot with a /setlimit command. Weekly and monthly limits are also available. A /stats command shows your total ticket spend, win/loss history, and net position over 7-day and 30-day periods.

Self-exclusion from lottery draws is available via the /cooloff command, with options for 24 hours, 7 days, or 30 days. During a cool-off period, the bot rejects any ticket purchase attempts and sends a reminder of the exclusion period. Permanent self-exclusion requires contacting support directly. The platform lacks some of the more sophisticated tools found on web-based platforms (no session time limits, no reality check popups), but the Telegram-native approach does make spending tracking accessible through simple commands. Links to GamCare and the National Council on Problem Gambling are pinned in the support channel.
