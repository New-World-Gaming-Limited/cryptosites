Regulatory Landscape in United States

The United States has the most complex crypto exchange regulatory environment in the world. Exchanges must register with FinCEN as Money Services Businesses, comply with Bank Secrecy Act requirements, and obtain state-level money transmitter licences — a process that can involve 50+ separate applications. Only a handful of exchanges have achieved full multi-state compliance.

The SEC's ongoing approach to classifying certain cryptocurrencies as securities has created additional uncertainty. Exchanges that list tokens deemed to be securities could face enforcement actions. This has led compliant exchanges like Coinbase and Kraken to be more conservative in their listing decisions, while offshore exchanges like Binance offer a wider (but potentially riskier) selection.

In 2026, the regulatory landscape is evolving. The FIT21 Act, which passed the House in 2024, continues to work through the legislative process and could provide clearer delineation between the SEC and CFTC's jurisdiction over different types of digital assets. Several states, notably Wyoming and Texas, have enacted crypto-friendly legislation that provides additional clarity for businesses and consumers. For US traders, prioritising exchanges with clear regulatory standing reduces counterparty risk significantly.

Tax Implications

All crypto transactions in the US are taxable events reported to the IRS. Short-term capital gains (held less than 1 year) are taxed as ordinary income. Long-term gains benefit from reduced rates (0%, 15%, or 20% depending on income). Exchanges like Coinbase and Kraken issue 1099 forms to the IRS. Use crypto tax software (CoinTracker, Koinly, TaxBit) to track cost basis accurately. Staking rewards and airdrops are taxed as ordinary income upon receipt.

VPN Usage: Risks & Considerations

Using a VPN to access global exchanges from the US is strongly discouraged. Exchanges like Binance (global) actively detect and ban US-based accounts. If discovered, your funds could be frozen and access revoked. US traders should use regulated US-compliant platforms that offer full legal protection and clear withdrawal guarantees.

Popular Payment Methods in United States

ACH bank transfers are the most cost-effective deposit method at US exchanges (free on Coinbase and Kraken). Wire transfers enable higher limits for active traders. Debit cards provide instant deposits but with higher fees (typically 1.5-3.5%). Apple Pay and Google Pay are supported by some platforms. Avoid credit card purchases due to cash advance fees charged by most card issuers.
