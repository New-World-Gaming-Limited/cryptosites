Overview

Gemini is a US-based cryptocurrency exchange founded in 2014 by Tyler and Cameron Winklevoss. Regulated by the New York Department of Financial Services (NYDFS), Gemini is one of the most compliant and security-focused exchanges in the industry. The platform prioritizes regulatory compliance, institutional-grade security, and a clean user experience.

While Gemini's fee structure is higher and its coin selection smaller than some competitors, the exchange excels in trust, security, and ease of use. The platform offers spot trading, ActiveTrader (advanced trading), Gemini Earn, a credit card with crypto rewards, and the Nifty Gateway NFT marketplace. For US-based users who prioritize regulation and security, Gemini is a top choice.

Fees & Promotions

Gemini occasionally offers new user promotions including free Bitcoin for completing your first trade. The Gemini credit card offers up to 3% back in crypto on purchases — effectively a continuous rewards program.

The Gemini Earn feature allows users to earn interest on their crypto holdings, though rates vary and terms should be carefully reviewed.

Trading Features

Gemini offers spot trading for 100+ cryptocurrencies through both its simple buy/sell interface and the ActiveTrader platform. ActiveTrader provides advanced charting, multiple order types, and block trading for institutional needs.

Additional products include Gemini Earn (interest on holdings), the Gemini credit card, custody services for institutions, and Nifty Gateway (NFT marketplace). The platform is more focused on quality and compliance than breadth of features.

Supported Cryptos & Fiat

Gemini supports 100+ cryptocurrencies, carefully curated to meet regulatory requirements. While the selection is smaller than exchanges like Binance or KuCoin, all listed assets have undergone thorough compliance review.

Fiat deposits are available via bank transfer (ACH), wire transfer, and debit card. As a US-regulated exchange, Gemini offers FDIC-insured USD deposits up to the applicable limit.

Security & Regulation

Gemini's security is among the strongest in the industry. The exchange holds a BitLicense from NYDFS, undergoes regular SOC 2 Type 2 audits, and maintains detailed insurance coverage. All crypto assets are stored in a combination of online and offline cold storage with multi-sig technology.

Gemini was the first exchange to complete a SOC 2 examination, demonstrating institutional-grade security controls. The platform's custodial arm, Gemini Custody, serves major institutional clients.

Mobile Experience

Gemini offers a clean, well-designed mobile app for iOS and Android. The app is particularly user-friendly for beginners, with a simple buy/sell interface. ActiveTrader features are also available on mobile for more advanced users.

Customer Support

Customer support is available via email and live chat. Gemini also offers phone support for certain account issues. The help center is well-organized with detailed guides. Support quality is generally high, reflecting the platform's premium positioning.

Fee Structure & Tiers

Gemini operates two distinct fee schedules depending on which interface you use — and the difference is substantial. On the standard consumer interface (the default web and app experience), Gemini charges a convenience fee that can reach 1.49% per transaction, plus a fixed fee on smaller orders. For a $100 purchase, you are looking at a $2.99 fee — nearly triple what you would pay on Coinbase Advanced Trade or Kraken Pro. This is the fee structure most retail users encounter without realising a cheaper option exists.

The real value is unlocked through ActiveTrader, Gemini's professional trading interface. Here, the exchange operates a standard maker/taker model: makers pay 0.20% and takers pay 0.40% at the base tier. Volume discounts kick in at $500,000 in 30-day trading volume, dropping maker fees to 0.15% and taker fees to 0.35%, with the top institutional tier (above $500 million monthly volume) bringing fees down to 0.03% maker and 0.10% taker. In our testing, switching from the consumer UI to ActiveTrader cut our effective fee rate by roughly 65% on a $1,000 ETH purchase. Compared to competitors — Kraken Pro starts at 0.16%/0.26% maker/taker, Binance at 0.10%/0.10% — Gemini's ActiveTrader fees are competitive for mid-tier traders but not the cheapest in the market.

Deposit and withdrawal fees vary by method. ACH transfers are free for both deposits and withdrawals, though ACH withdrawals are limited to $100,000 per day. Wire transfers carry a $10 fee for domestic wires. Crypto withdrawals are free up to 10 transactions per month, after which a network fee applies. One notable advantage: Gemini does not inflate withdrawal fees above actual on-chain costs, which is a practice some competing exchanges have been criticised for. For users who move crypto frequently, this adds up to meaningful savings over time.

Trading Pairs & Liquidity

Gemini lists approximately 120 trading pairs as of March 2026, primarily denominated in USD, GUSD (Gemini Dollar), BTC, and ETH. The selection is deliberately conservative — Gemini's regulatory approval process means assets go through extensive legal review before listing. You will find all major assets: BTC, ETH, SOL, DOGE, MATIC, AVAX, LINK, ADA, and stablecoins including USDT, USDC, and the native GUSD. What you will not find is the long tail of speculative altcoins available on Binance or KuCoin, and there are no perpetual futures or leveraged tokens.

Liquidity on core BTC/USD and ETH/USD pairs is solid. In our testing, a $50,000 market order on BTC/USD filled with less than 0.05% slippage during US trading hours — comparable to Coinbase Advanced Trade for large orders. Order book depth on mid-cap pairs like SOL/USD and AVAX/USD is thinner, and we observed 0.15–0.20% slippage on $10,000 AVAX market orders during off-peak hours. For institutional block trades above $100,000, Gemini's block trading desk offers negotiated pricing that effectively bypasses the public order book, which is a significant advantage for funds executing large positions without moving the market.

Cross-pair liquidity is more limited than on centralised exchanges with larger international user bases. ETH/BTC has reasonable depth, but pairs like SOL/ETH or LINK/BTC show noticeably wider spreads — often 0.3–0.5% at the mid price during quiet sessions. Traders focused primarily on BTC and ETH against USD will find Gemini's liquidity more than sufficient. Anyone building a diversified altcoin position across 10+ assets would be better served supplementing with a higher-liquidity venue for the smaller-cap trades.

Advanced Trading Features

ActiveTrader is Gemini's answer to professional-grade execution. The platform supports limit orders, market orders, stop-limit orders, immediate-or-cancel (IOC), and fill-or-kill (FOK) order types. The charting interface is powered by TradingView, giving traders access to the full suite of technical indicators — RSI, MACD, Bollinger Bands, volume profile, and over 100 additional studies. In our testing, the charting tools loaded quickly and handled real-time tick data without lag, even during high-volatility periods. The interface is cleaner and more stable than Bybit's, though it lacks Binance's depth-of-market visualisation and heatmap overlays.

What Gemini does not offer is equally important to note: there are no leveraged products, no perpetual futures, no options, and no margin trading for US residents. This is a direct consequence of regulatory positioning — NYDFS rules constrain the products Gemini can offer to retail users. Compared to Kraken, which offers up to 5x margin on select pairs for eligible US clients, or Coinbase Advanced Trade with its futures products, Gemini is firmly a spot-only exchange for the vast majority of users. Professional institutional clients can access OTC block trading and custody, but the retail derivatives gap is a genuine limitation for active traders who manage directional risk with hedging instruments.

The ActiveTrader API is a strong point. Gemini offers a REST API and WebSocket API with market data, order management, and account endpoints. Rate limits are generous — 600 private API requests per minute — and the WebSocket feed provides sub-100ms latency for order book updates in our testing. The API documentation is thorough and well-maintained, and Gemini provides a sandbox environment for strategy testing. We found the API more reliable than Coinbase's during high-traffic periods, with no observable rate limiting during the March 2026 BTC rally. For algorithmic traders who need a compliant, US-regulated venue, Gemini's API infrastructure is a genuine differentiator.

Staking & Earn Products

Gemini Earn, the platform's crypto lending product, was paused in November 2022 following the collapse of Genesis Global Capital — the counterparty that managed Gemini Earn's loan book. This left approximately 340,000 Earn users with locked funds, and the subsequent legal proceedings stretched into 2024 before a settlement was reached. By early 2025, Gemini had returned capital to affected Earn users. The episode was a significant reputational and operational setback, and it underscores the counterparty risk inherent in any yield-bearing crypto product that relies on third-party lending.

As of March 2026, Gemini has relaunched a more limited version of staking for proof-of-stake assets. Supported staking assets include ETH, SOL, and MATIC, with ETH staking currently offering approximately 3.2% APY — in line with the broader liquid staking market but below what Lido or Rocket Pool offer through self-custody staking solutions. The key trade-off is simplicity: staking through Gemini requires no technical setup, withdrawal delays are manageable (typically 3–7 days for ETH unstaking), and rewards are distributed automatically to your exchange balance. For users who value convenience over maximising yield, it is a reasonable option.

Gemini also offers the Gemini Credit Card, which provides up to 3% back in cryptocurrency on eligible purchases — the highest crypto cashback rate among major exchange-issued cards as of this writing. Rewards are paid in BTC by default, but users can select any supported asset. We found that the card's rewards structure is straightforward, with no annual fee on the base tier, though the premium tiers (Platinum, Brilliant) carry annual fees of $99 and $249 respectively. Compared to the BlockFi card (discontinued) and the Coinbase Card (1–4% back), the Gemini card holds its own as a passive accumulation tool for existing Gemini users.

Regulatory Status & Insurance

Gemini's regulatory standing is the strongest of any US-based exchange. The company operates as a New York Trust Company under the supervision of the New York Department of Financial Services — a licence that imposes bank-level compliance obligations including capital adequacy requirements, AML/KYC mandates, cybersecurity standards, and regular examinations. This is a materially higher regulatory bar than a money transmitter licence (which most US exchanges hold) and explains why Gemini was able to obtain NYDFS approval before competitors. The exchange also holds FCA registration in the United Kingdom and licences in several other jurisdictions, allowing it to serve clients in 60+ countries.

On the security and insurance front, Gemini was the first exchange to complete a SOC 2 Type 2 examination — an independent audit of security, availability, and confidentiality controls. The audit is conducted annually, and reports are available to institutional clients on request. Gemini holds insurance coverage for digital assets held in its online (hot) storage through a syndicate of Lloyd's of London underwriters, with coverage limits disclosed upon request to institutional clients. Cold storage assets — which represent the majority of customer holdings — are segregated from company funds and protected by Gemini's own balance sheet in the event of a loss. USD deposits held in fiat are FDIC-insured up to $250,000 per depositor through partner banks.

Compared to competitors, Gemini's insurance and regulatory posture is clearly best-in-class among US exchanges. Coinbase holds similar regulatory licences and SOC 2 certification, but Coinbase's insurance disclosures are less detailed. Kraken holds money transmitter licences in most US states but not a New York Trust Company charter. Binance.US has faced ongoing regulatory scrutiny from the SEC and CFTC. For institutional allocators with fiduciary obligations — pension funds, family offices, registered investment advisers — Gemini's regulatory infrastructure frequently makes it the only viable US exchange from a compliance standpoint, a competitive position that is unlikely to erode regardless of fee or feature competition from less-regulated peers.
