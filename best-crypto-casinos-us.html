Regulatory Landscape in United States

The United States has no federal law that explicitly prohibits individuals from playing at offshore crypto casinos. However, the legal landscape is complex and varies significantly by state. The Wire Act of 1961 restricts interstate wagering, and the Unlawful Internet Gambling Enforcement Act (UIGEA) of 2006 targets payment processors rather than individual players.

In practice, most crypto casinos operate from offshore jurisdictions (Curacao, Malta, or Costa Rica) and accept US players, though they rarely hold US-specific licences. Individual states like New Jersey, Pennsylvania, and Michigan have legalised regulated online gambling but only through state-licensed operators — crypto-native casinos typically fall outside these frameworks.

As of 2026, the regulatory trend is toward greater clarity. Wyoming and Colorado have introduced blockchain-friendly legislation that could eventually encompass crypto gambling. The IRS has also tightened crypto reporting requirements, meaning players should maintain records of gambling transactions for tax purposes regardless of the platform's licensing status.

Tax Implications

The IRS treats crypto gambling winnings as taxable income. If you win, the fair market value of the crypto at the time of receipt is considered ordinary income. Losses can be deducted against winnings (but not below zero). You should keep detailed records of deposits, withdrawals, wins, and losses. Some platforms provide transaction histories that can be exported for tax reporting. Consult a tax professional familiar with crypto for specific guidance.

VPN Usage: Risks & Considerations

Some players use VPNs to access platforms that geo-restrict US users. This carries significant risks: platforms may void winnings, freeze accounts, or refuse withdrawals if VPN usage is detected. Additionally, it likely violates the platform's terms of service. If you choose to gamble, we strongly recommend using platforms that explicitly accept US players rather than circumventing restrictions.
