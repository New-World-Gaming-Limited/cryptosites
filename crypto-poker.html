How Crypto Poker Rooms Differ

Crypto poker rooms use Bitcoin, Ethereum, or other cryptocurrencies at the tables instead of fiat. This means faster deposits, faster cashouts, and in many cases lower rake. Some rooms like CoinPoker and SWC Poker are crypto-native, while others like GGPoker and Americas Cardroom accept crypto alongside traditional payment methods.

Rake and Rakeback Compared

Rake is the fee the poker room takes from each pot. CoinPoker charges 2-5% capped at reasonable levels and returns 33% weekly as rakeback. SWC Poker uses a lower rake structure suited to micro and small stakes. GGPoker's Fish Buffet loyalty program converts rakeback into randomized rewards, while ACR offers 27% baseline rakeback with up to 65% through the Elite Benefits program.

Tournament Value at Crypto Poker Rooms

CoinPoker runs the Crypto Series of Online Poker (CSOP) with guaranteed prize pools exceeding $100K per month. ACR is known for its $5M guaranteed tournament series. GGPoker offers the largest tournament guarantees globally, though their crypto integration is secondary to their fiat operations.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best crypto poker site?

GGPoker leads on traffic and tournament guarantees. CoinPoker is the best crypto-native option with 33% rakeback. Americas Cardroom offers the most established crypto poker experience.

Is crypto poker legal?

Crypto poker legality varies by jurisdiction. Offshore sites like CoinPoker and SWC Poker accept players from most countries. GGPoker operates under multiple licenses and has country-specific restrictions.

Do crypto poker rooms have good traffic?

GGPoker has the highest traffic globally. CoinPoker has medium traffic with peak hours during European evenings. SWC Poker is smaller but attracts dedicated crypto enthusiasts.

What is rakeback in crypto poker?

Rakeback is the percentage of rake (house fee) returned to you. CoinPoker offers 33% weekly rakeback. ACR offers 27% baseline with up to 65% through VIP tiers.

Can I play poker anonymously with crypto?

SWC Poker and Blockchain Poker allow anonymous play without KYC. CoinPoker requires basic verification. GGPoker and ACR require full KYC.
